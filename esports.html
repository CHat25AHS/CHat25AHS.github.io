Altoona High School Esports!

What is Esports?

Esports are multiplayer online games played on a competitive level, often times for spectators to watch. For example, some Esports played in Altoona are Super Smash Bros Ultimate and Mario Kart 8 Deluxe. On a school level, competitions are played versus other schools and schools can have multiple teams to account for multiple students. The site that Altoona High School (AHS) uses for their Esports is PlayVS, this helps keep track of teams, who we are competing against, and more! The schools you are playing are split up by region, Centeral, Eastern, and Pacific are the regions for the US area.

How are Meetings structured?

There are 2 types of meetings for the AHS Esports team, practices and game days. Practice days take place on Mondays and Tuesdays after school from 4pm to 5pm. Mondays and Tuesdays are different for the 2 games we have, Mondays is for Super Smash Bros Ultimate and Tuesdays are for Mario Kart. On these days you just play the game for the day to get some more pracice for game days. Wednesdays and Thursdays are Gamedays, and they go from 4pm to 5:30pm after school to account for the competition times. On Wednesday, it is a Super Smash Bros Ultimate game day and on Thursday it is a Mario Kart 8 Deluxe game day. On these days you will compete against another school in the format for PlayVS.

How are Competitions Played?

Super Smash Bros Ultimate

Super Smaash Bros Ultimate competitions go as follows, you play a best of 5 sets with 3 players on each team. For the each set, each team will send in their first player, you will go through a process of confirming your character and selecting stages through stage striking. After that the selected players will play a match, each player gets 3 lives each round and after each round, you will report the winner. Each set requires a best of 3 rounds and after you are done, the next player on your team fights another player from the other team. Each set continues like that until one team wins 3 sets.

Mario Kart 8 Deluxe

Mario Kart 8 Deluxe is a very different game than Super Smash Bros Ultimate, so it requires a different rules set. Teams will play a best of 3 sets, with each set containg 6 races. The winner of each set is decided by which team has the most points at the end of the 6 races. Each team has 4 racers and everyone has to select random tracks, the game rules must be set to normal items and a team game. The first team to win 2 sets will win the competition.
